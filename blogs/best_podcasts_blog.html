The Best Podcasts For a Sane Quarantine

As we approach the 6th month of limited social activity I wanted to share the most interesting podcasts that have helped me stay sane throughout these unprecedented times.

Daniel Shoichet

July 12, 2020

A medium of information that has increasingly appealed to me over the past several months has been podcasts. I believe that this is an underappreciated form of media that could help teenagers and young adults to make sense of this confusing world. As you go through this list I have compiled keep in mind that these are only my preferences and any questions or comments are always welcome in the comment section.

Joe Rogan Experience

I will preface this list by saying that my favourite Joe Rogan Experience (JRE) episodes have been the ones that changed my opinions on a wide range of issues. These episodes have influenced my outlook on the world and without a doubt shine through at any philosophical or political debate with peers.

1169 - Elon Musk
  • Probably my favourite single podcast episode of all time. Significant credit goes to Joe Rogan for a phenomenal interview with one of the most interesting minds of our era. (This is the episode where Elon smokes weed on a livestream).
1006 - Jordan Peterson and Bret Weinstein
  • An incredibly insightful JRE episode with perspectives from two incredibly intelligent individuals. They discuss university bias, the holocaust and Hitler, and additional issues that resulted in a very interesting discussion.
1258 - Jack Dorsey, Vijaya Gadde and Tim Pool
  • Jack Dorsey, CEO of Twitter brought along Twitter executive Vijaya Gadde to answer questions from journalist Tim Pool about the company. First, huge respects to Jack for agreeing to participate in this interview. This episode showed the world the (often untold) perspective of social media companies in post-2016 America. This episode will undeniably change your perspective on the role of social media in society.
974 - Megan-Phelps-Roper
  • An incredible interview with a former member of the controversial Westboro Baptist Church. Megan has a perspective that few people poses after her bizarre interactions with the people around her, her community, and the internet.
1080 - David Goggins
  • Wow. Certainly the most motivational podcast I have ever listened to. Goggins is a retired navy seal, an ultramarathon runner and amongst other skills an incredible motivational speaker. A fascinating story that just gets crazier and crazier as Goggins explains his life and his route to personal success.
1368 - Edward Snowden
  • A very insightful history lesson from Uncle Snowden about how American intelligence changed post 9-11. It is great to hear the story from his perspective and not from a government institution nor internet conspiracy theorists.
1265 - Andrew Schultz
  • I am generally not that attracted to comedians on Joe’s show but since I already knew Schultz from his comedies I decided to give him a listen. I ended up learning more about Joe Rogan on this podcast than I expected. Schultz is a very smart guy with interesting takes on issues. Many of his videos regarding political issues recently went viral on Instagram and YouTube.

Jocko Podcast

A fantastic podcast by former navy seal lieutenant commander Jocko Willink. Jocko popularized the phrase “Discipline equals Freedom” with an addictive podcast format that makes every new episode interesting and exciting. He frequently interviews people with fascinating life stories that have gone through insane paths to get to where they are today.

221 - Jonny Kim
  • My favourite episode of Jocko’s podcast. Jonny is a former navy seal, a doctor from Harvard medical school, and most recently a Nasa astronaut. Too many people in society do not even dream of achieving even one of these accomplishments yet Kim, with fantastic storytelling, explains what it took for him to excel at everything he does. This epic 5-hour podcast is sure to inspire any and all listeners.

Dan Carlin's Hardcore History

Arguably the best history podcast available on the internet. I have spent over 100 hours on many episodes learning what high school history class could only dream of teaching me. Dan Carlin made learning history fun and incredibly interesting. Regardless how controversial and divisive the event, Carlin can always take an objective view of the subject at hand.

Blueprint for Armageddon
  • An epic six-episode trek into the first world war. I would truly recommend this to anyone regardless of their interest in the era. Far too many people say, “World War 2 is more interesting that World War 1,” but the way Dan Carlin explains this conflict will undoubtedly change their perspective.
Ghosts of the Ostfront
  • This four-part series is focused on the Western front of the second world war between the Soviet Union and Nazi Germany. A highly informative series that somehow manages to put in perspective the atrocities committed by both sides in this part of the war.
The destroyer of worlds
  • A very informative podcast about geopolitics of early nuclear technology. I would recommend however to start with Blueprint for Armageddon and Ghosts of the Ostfront.

Martyrmade

Another fantastic history podcast! This podcast focuses on more modern events and issues yet addresses them with the same detail and level of research as Dan Carlin's Hardcore History.

Fear & Loathing in the New Jerusalem
  • A mostly unbiased summary of major events that lead to the creation of the state of Israel. Darryl Cooper tells a fascinating story that not many people are aware of yet adore arguing about. Recommended for anyone looking to learn more about middle eastern and Israeli history.
God's Socialist
  • A fascinating overview of Jim Jones and People's Temple. Daryl Cooper does a phenomenal job researching and explaining the political and cultural climate of the 60s. You will quickly get immersed in this chaotic and convoluted world that is in too many ways similar to the one we live in today.

Reply All

It has been a while since I binged on Reply All simply because I am more attracted to longer form podcasts, yet I would highly recommend reply all to anyone interested in beginning their podcast journey.

102, 103 - Long Distance parts 1 and 2
  • An epic investigative journalism adventure into the Indian call Centre scamming industry. While this may not seem like an interesting subject to most, we have all at some point wondered who is behind the robocall that demands we pay a fortune to the “CRA” or face legal penalties for tax evasion. These episodes will instantly spark interest in a world few people know exists. This was the first podcast I listened to after online recommendations (mostly reddit) and it is a no brainer for anyone interested in exploring this medium.
127, 128 - The crime machine parts 1 and 2
  • An eye-opening episode about second order consequences of a seemingly effective method of policing. Especially relevant now with increased public pressure on municipalities to cut police budgets.
130 – The Snapchat Thief
  • This episode follows a stolen snapchat account through the darkest parts of the web and along the way discovers many flaws with the ways we depend on our online accounts. After listening to this episode, I began using a password manager and 2-factor authentication for all my internet accounts.

T.A.A.A.A.A Podcast

I must show some support for my boys Jaron and Gavin from Winnipeg. It is always great to hear more perspectives about local issues that are not discussed in the other podcasts in this list. Give these guys a listen and you will not regret it!

Radiolab

The Other Latif
  • An incredibly informative and interesting investigative series of episodes that uncovers the life of Guantanamo bay detainee 244, Abdul Latif Nasser. Amazing storytelling by the Radiolab team which changed my views of certain post 9-11 American policies. Recommended for anyone interested in learning a new perspective on the American war on terror.

Black Box Down

A fascinating aviation podcast that investigates commercial airline incidents. I would say to avoid this podcast while flying but without a doubt listen to it on the ground. This is a relatively new podcast with only a number of episodes, and while some airline incidents are more interesting than others, I would suggest to begin with episode one.
