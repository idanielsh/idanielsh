Why I Use Light Mode

While this explanation for a peculiar preference won't please many people, I realize that this is my best opportunity to share with the world my argument.

Daniel Shoichet

May 25, 2020

Since my early days at the University of Toronto I have had to defend myself passionately and vigorously anytime there is someone around me and I open any IDE, discord, Gmail, etc. Seeing as I now have an online presence under my name, I see no better opportunity to share with the rest of the world my reasoning for using light mode.

Ever since humans ceased writing on rocks thousands of years ago, we have shifted to write on lighter fabrics. If dark mode was as superior to light mode as many claim, wouldn’t have the ancient Egyptians tainted their papyrus scrolls with a darker material? We must build off the knowledge of historic cultures to progress as a human race.

Do you ever look at a paper and think to yourself, “wouldn’t it be nice if this paper was black?” No, no one does, so I do not see the reasoning as to why digital content should be dark. Interactions with computers should mirror interaction with pen and paper. Afterall, everyone knows that reading a book is far superior to reading on a digital screen.

Many people believe dark mode is easier to use at night, but I would argue that most dark mode themes do not have adequate contrast even during the day (cough cough jetbrains darcula). What this results in is greater eye strain and reduced productivity late at night.

One might propose the argument that in our modern OLED screens it is more battery efficient to use dark colors as much as possible. To that I argue that in 2020, our devices have large enough batteries to easily sustain full day use in light mode.

So, to conclude this entry I urge all developers (Spotify, Netflix et al) to implement light modes whenever possible. ■

All jokes aside, please use whatever colour theme you find more visually appealing. De gustibus non est disputandum.

Disclaimer: I was not in any way bullied for using light mode.
